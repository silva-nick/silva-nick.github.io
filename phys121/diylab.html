Part 1: Theory

Harmonic Motion

To begin, we will start to find an answer to our first fundamental question: Is the ball's motion harmonic? Harmonic motion is oscillatory movement caused by an external force proportional to distance from the center. When dealing with springs, we used Hooke's law to describe that restoring force (Fs=−kxF_s=-kxFs​=−kx). Using this we can find the springs potential energy: PEspring=1/2kx2PE_{spring}=1/2kx^2PEspring​=1/2kx2. Therefore, we can also describe harmonic motion as a situation where potential energy is proportional to the distance from the center squared. For the ball in the parabola, we have:
PEball=mgy=mgkx2PE_{ball}=mgy=mgkx^2PEball​=mgy=mgkx2
Therefore, the balls restoring force is proportional to the x distance away it is from the mean.

Part 3: Discussion

Conclusions

Sources of Error

  1. The balls rotational motion. As stated above, the ball's motion along the ramp alternated between rotation and sliding. This difference changes the way we understand and describe the balls movement. Therefore, were I to redo the experiment I would either: (1) Find a ball and ramp with higher friction so that it was rolling at all times or (2) Use a low friction object that doesn't roll.
  2. Using a video to capture data points. This procedure was time comsuming and inaccurate. Differences in the camera's angle, position, and lense may influence the data collected later.
